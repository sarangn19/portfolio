Context

A self-initiated UI exploration

This project focused on improving how users browse, evaluate, and purchase fashion products online. The goal was to balance aesthetic appeal with fast decision-making — a tension that's central to fashion ecommerce.

EpicSix is a modern apparel brand with strong visual identity. The design challenge was to let the products speak while keeping the experience frictionless from discovery to checkout.

Focus Areas

Three things that mattered most

🔍
Product discovery — fast scanning and filtering so users find what they want without friction.
🎯
Decision clarity — visual hierarchy and product information that builds confidence, not confusion.
🛒
Conversion flow — reducing every unnecessary step between intent and purchase.
Browse Experience

Designed for fast scanning

The product listing page uses a clean, evenly spaced grid so users can compare options at a glance. Neutral backgrounds keep attention on the product visuals, and persistent left-side filters let users refine results without losing their browsing context.

Key Design Decisions

Where the thinking shows

01 — Discovery
Grid designed for fast scanning
Products are displayed in a clean, evenly spaced grid to help users compare options quickly without cognitive overload.
02 — Focus
Minimal distractions, strong product focus
Neutral backgrounds and consistent card layouts ensure attention stays on the product visuals — not the UI chrome.
03 — Control
Left-side filtering for persistent control
Persistent filters allow users to refine results without losing browsing context — no page reloads, no lost scroll position.
04 — Interaction
Lightweight interaction layer
Wishlist and quick-add actions are accessible directly from the grid without opening product pages, reducing friction for high-intent users.
05 — Conversion
Clear purchase hierarchy on product pages
Primary actions (Add to Cart / Buy Now) are visually dominant on the product page, making the next step obvious and reducing hesitation.
Product Page

Designed for quick, confident decisions

The product page is built around one goal: helping users decide fast without feeling rushed. Every element earns its place.

🖼️
Large imagery builds trust — multiple angles give users confidence before committing.
📐
Size selection is frictionless — clear size chips with no dropdowns or extra steps.
📋
Key information is scannable — price, fit, and material are visible without scrolling.
📦
Supporting details are available but not intrusive — full description is present for those who want it, but doesn't block the purchase path.
Learnings

What this project taught me

Visual hierarchy directly impacts conversion — where the eye goes first determines what action gets taken.
Reducing decision friction in ecommerce is more valuable than adding features. Every extra step is a drop-off risk.
Clarity beats creativity in high-intent flows. When someone wants to buy, the design should get out of the way.
Persistent filters are underrated — they give users a sense of control that keeps them browsing longer.
