Context

Designing for a fashion-forward audience

EpicSix is a fashion ecommerce UI exploration focused on creating a premium, conversion-optimised shopping experience. The challenge was to balance strong visual identity with clear product communication and a frictionless purchase flow.

Focus Areas

Three things this design had to nail

?
Conversion flow � reducing friction from product view to add-to-cart to checkout.
Browse Experience

Making product discovery feel effortless

The homepage leads with a strong hero that communicates the brand's aesthetic immediately. Product listings use a clean grid with hover states that reveal key details without cluttering the initial view.

Sticky navigation with category filters keeps browsing context clear at all times.
Product cards show price, name, and a quick-add option on hover � reducing clicks to purchase.
Editorial sections break up the grid and reinforce brand storytelling between product rows.
Key Design Decisions

Where the thinking shows

01
Full-bleed hero with minimal text
The hero communicates brand identity through imagery first. Text is kept minimal � a tagline and a single CTA � to avoid competing with the visual.
02
Consistent product card anatomy
Every card follows the same structure: image, name, price, colour options. Consistency reduces cognitive load and speeds up browsing decisions.
03
Product page built for confidence
Large imagery, size guide, material details, and reviews are all above the fold. Users have everything they need to decide without scrolling.
04
Sticky add-to-cart on product page
The CTA follows the user as they scroll through product details � removing the need to scroll back up to purchase.
05
Related products placed post-decision
Cross-sell recommendations appear after the add-to-cart section, not before � so they do not distract from the primary conversion action.
Learnings

What this project taught me

In fashion ecommerce, the image is the product. Every layout decision should serve the photography, not compete with it.
Conversion and aesthetics are not opposites � the best ecommerce design achieves both by being clear and beautiful simultaneously.
Micro-decisions like hover states and sticky CTAs have an outsized impact on how effortless the purchase flow feels.
