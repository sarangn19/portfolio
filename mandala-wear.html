Context

When craft needs a voice

Mandala Wear sells handcrafted clothing rooted in Indian textile traditions. The challenge was that their products carry deep cultural meaning — but their ecommerce experience treated them like any other fashion item. The design needed to bridge that gap.

Core Problem

The story was missing from the shopping experience

Handcrafted products carry meaning that mass-produced items do not. When that meaning is absent from the shopping experience, the product becomes just another item competing on price. Mandala Wear needed a design that made the story inseparable from the product.

Solution Approach

Story-first, product-second

??
Craft origin stories — each product page opens with the story of the textile tradition it comes from.
??
Artisan profiles — connecting buyers to the makers behind each piece builds trust and emotional investment.
??
Material transparency — detailed breakdowns of fabrics, dyes, and techniques reinforce authenticity.
Key Design Thinking

Decisions that shaped the experience

01
Editorial homepage over product grid
The homepage leads with a story — a featured textile tradition, its origin, and the artisans behind it — before showing any products. This sets the emotional context for everything that follows.
02
Product pages structured as narratives
Instead of the standard image-price-size layout, product pages follow a story arc: origin ? craft process ? artisan ? product details ? purchase. The CTA comes after the story, not before.
03
Warm, earthy visual language
The colour palette, typography, and photography style all reflect the handcrafted, natural quality of the products — avoiding the sterile look of typical fashion ecommerce.
04
UX writing as a design tool
Every label, button, and description was written to reinforce the brand's voice — warm, knowledgeable, and respectful of the craft. "Add to bag" became "Bring this home."
05
Collection pages organised by tradition
Instead of filtering by colour or size first, collections are organised by textile tradition — Ajrakh, Kalamkari, Ikat — helping users discover through cultural interest, not just product attributes.
UX Writing Strategy

Language that carries the brand

Product CTA language
How word choice changes the emotional register
Generic

"Add to Cart" — transactional, cold, no connection to the product's meaning or the brand's values.

Mandala Wear

"Bring this home" — warm, intentional, and consistent with the idea that buying handcraft is a meaningful act, not just a transaction.

Learnings

What this project taught me

UX writing is not a finishing touch — it is a core design decision that shapes how users feel about a product.
Emotional engagement and conversion are not in tension. When users connect with a story, they are more likely to buy.
Cultural context requires research. Designing for a tradition you do not understand risks misrepresentation.
If I Had More Time

What I would explore next

Artisan portal
A dedicated space for artisans to share updates, new work, and their stories directly with buyers.
Care & longevity guide
Handcrafted textiles need specific care. An in-product guide would reinforce the value of the purchase.
Community features
User-generated content showing how buyers style and wear their pieces — building social proof organically.
