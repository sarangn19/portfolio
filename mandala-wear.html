Mandala Wear — Story-driven Ecommerce Experience

Mandala Wear — full experience overview

Context

Ecommerce that goes beyond transactions

This project explores how ecommerce can integrate culture, storytelling, and scarcity-driven design into the shopping experience. The goal was to make each product feel meaningful — not just purchasable.

Core Problem

Products that feel interchangeable

Most fashion ecommerce platforms focus on price and visuals but fail to create emotional connection — making products feel interchangeable and forgettable.

Solution Approach

A system built on meaning

I designed a system that combines three layers to shift the experience from transactional to emotional.

🎨 Cultural storytelling (Kerala mural art) 🔥 Scarcity & drop mechanics 🛒 Frictionless commerce flows
Key Design Thinking

Where the real work is

01 — Narrative
Product as a story, not just an item
Each product is positioned as a narrative: "More than a T-shirt. A Story." This shifts user perception from buying clothing to owning meaning.
02 — Urgency
Scarcity-driven behaviour design
Urgency cues used without aggressive sales tactics:
  • "Limited batch"
  • "Only a few left"
  • "Drops every week"
03 — Discovery
Smart search & discovery experience
Contextual search suggestions (Ganesh, Krishna, Mandala), category shortcuts, and trending indicators reduce effort and speed up product discovery.
04 — Cart
Low-friction add-to-cart experience
Inline size selection before adding, quick "Added to Cart" feedback, and persistent purchase visibility — designed to minimise drop-offs at intent moments.
05 — Product Page
Confidence-driven product page
Answers key user questions fast: fit clarity ("Slightly relaxed fit"), cultural context (story behind the artwork), and social proof (ratings + buyers).
06 — Checkout
Optimised checkout for Indian users
UPI-first payment (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), Cash on Delivery option, and clear pricing breakdown — built for trust and familiarity in Indian ecommerce behaviour.
07 — Flow
Guided step-based checkout
Cart → Delivery → Payment → Confirmation. A structured flow that reduces confusion and improves completion rates.
08 — Post-purchase
Experience beyond the transaction
Order confirmation with clarity, delivery tracking, and an emotional follow-up — "We'll share the story of this artwork" — extends the brand experience after purchase.
UX Writing Strategy

Copy that reinforces the brand

Instead of generic ecommerce language, every line of copy is designed to feel cultural and narrative-driven.

Get ₹100 off → Share art. Earn stories.
Add to cart → Own this story
Limited stock → Limited batch — drops every week
Learnings

What this project taught me

Emotional storytelling can significantly influence purchase intent — people buy meaning, not just products.
Scarcity works best when it feels authentic, not manipulative. The framing matters as much as the mechanic.
Small UX writing changes can redefine the entire brand perception at every touchpoint.
