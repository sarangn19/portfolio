Case Study · 01 · Product Design

Transparent Checkout & Pricing System

Reducing price shocks and improving trust in food delivery checkout — a UX case study on pricing clarity.

Role UI/UX Designer
Domain Food Delivery · Mobile
Methods UX Research · Interaction Design
Tools Figma · FigJam
Year 2025
01 — Overview

Why does checkout feel like a trap?

Many food delivery platforms reveal additional charges only during the final stage of checkout. This creates a mismatch between the user's expected price and the actual payment amount, often leading to frustration, cart abandonment, and reduced trust.

The goal of this project was to design a checkout system that communicates clear pricing information earlier in the ordering journey — so users always know what they're paying before they reach the payment step.

Goals
  • Reduce price anxiety during checkout
  • Improve clarity of delivery fees and taxes
  • Build trust through transparent pricing
  • Help users understand the total order cost earlier
Expected Outcome
  • A checkout experience that allows users to understand how the final price is calculated before they reach the payment stage
02 — Problem

Users often feel blindsided at checkout.

Users often encounter unexpected price increases during checkout, due to charges that appear only at the final billing step — such as service fees, delivery surcharges, and taxes added late in the flow.

Key issues identified:

Visibility
  • Delivery and service fees appearing late in the process
  • Confusing fee structures with no clear breakdown
Trust
  • Lack of clear explanation for pricing components
  • Sudden price increases before payment that reduce user confidence

These issues frequently lead to frustration and increase the likelihood of users abandoning their orders.

03 — User Research

Understanding how users perceive pricing.

To understand how users perceive pricing during food delivery checkout, I conducted secondary research and analysed the existing checkout experience across multiple platforms. The goal was to identify where confusion occurs and how pricing transparency can improve user trust.

UX Pattern Analysis
Pricing patterns used in delivery models across platforms
Competitive Analysis
Checkout flows from Swiggy, Zomato, Uber Eats & GrubHub
User Interviews
8 participants, ages 20–35, 2–3 orders per week

Key User Behaviors observed:

Checkout SurpriseUsers often experience a sudden price change during checkout that causes hesitation and erodes trust in the platform.
Fee ConfusionMany users do not understand what service fees or platform fees represent, causing additional anxiety at checkout.
Decision HesitationUnexpected price increases reduce the likelihood that users will complete their order, leading to higher cart abandonment rates.

Key Findings: Users prefer seeing the estimated final cost earlier in their journey. Transparency significantly improves confidence during payment decisions. Sudden price increases during checkout reduce trust.

04 — Insights & Problem Statement

Pricing opacity is a trust killer.

From the research, several core insights emerged:

Insight A
  • Pricing transparency plays a major role in building user trust throughout the checkout experience
Insight B
  • Users feel frustrated when prices change suddenly at checkout — even when the final price is still reasonable
Insight C
  • Clear breakdowns help users understand what they are paying for, building confidence in the platform
Insight D
  • Early visibility of total cost reduces checkout friction and decreases cart abandonment rates significantly

Problem Statement: Food delivery users need a transparent pricing system that clearly communicates the total cost of their order before checkout, because hidden or unexpected fees create confusion and reduce trust in the platform.

05 — Solution & User Flow

Making every rupee visible, every step of the way.

The solution introduces a transparent pricing system that provides clear cost visibility throughout the entire ordering journey.

Visibility
  • Early display of estimated total price on item pages
  • Clear cost hierarchy for cost information
Breakdown
  • Detailed breakdown of delivery fees, service charges, and taxes
  • A condensed pricing summary across the checkout flow

06 — Design & Core Screens

Three principles guided every decision.

Clarity
Keep users informed at all times about what they are paying
Transparency
All charges must be visible and explained to the user
Predictability
Users should know the expected total cost before reaching checkout

Key Design Decisions:

Estimated Total in CartThe redesigned cart widget always helps users understand the full cost before checkout.
Fee Breakdown SectionA dedicated section explains delivery fees, service charges, and all taxes — no hidden surprises.
Cooldown Pricing SummaryMatching a stable price summary throughout checkout prevents confusion before the final payment step.
07 — Outcome & Learnings

Trust is built one transparent step at a time.

The redesigned checkout experience aims to improve pricing transparency and build user trust during the payment process.

↓ Cart
Abandonment reduced through earlier price visibility
↑ Trust
User confidence improved with full fee breakdown
↑ Clarity
Fee comprehension improved via dedicated cost summary

Key Learnings:

Pricing transparency builds trustSeeing pricing information early creates significantly more trust than revealing it at the final step.
Even small improvements matterSmall improvements in how cost information is communicated can significantly influence user behaviour and satisfaction.
Financial UX demands simplicityDesigning financial interactions requires balancing transparency with simplicity — too much data at once overwhelms users.

Reflection: This project reinforced the importance of designing from domain experience in digital products. Transparency doesn't just strengthen user confidence — it signals that the product experience is built with integrity. By making pricing more transparent and predictable, platforms can create smoother checkout experiences and improved customer trust.
